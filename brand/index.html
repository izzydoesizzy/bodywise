Brand
Guidelines

Find the wisdom in every cycle, enjoy vitality in every phase of life.

The visual and verbal identity of Bodywise: gender-expansive fertility awareness education, counselling, and workshops with Laura.

Version 1.0 July 2026 feelbodywise.com

01 Brand essence

Warm

Bodywise sounds like a knowledgeable friend, never a clinic. Soft colour, rounded shapes, generous space, and photography full of daylight and plants.

Empowering

The client is always in the driver's seat. The brand informs and supports; it never prescribes, pressures, or promises outcomes.

Inclusive

Explicitly gender-expansive. Bodywise speaks to all people with menstrual cycles, and the design avoids clichés of pink-for-women femininity while staying warm.

"It is the right of every person to know how their body works."

02 Logo

The wordmark & the cycle ring

The logo pairs a lowercase Poppins-style wordmark in Deep Plum with the cycle ring: sixteen dots alternating Warm Orange and Berry, representing the phases of a cycle. The ring may be used alone as an avatar, favicon, or accent.

bodywise

Do

  • Use the wordmark on Cream, White, Blush, or Sand backgrounds.
  • Use the cream recreation (text + ring) on Plum or Berry fields.
  • Keep clearspace around the logo equal to the ring's diameter.
  • Minimum wordmark width: 120 px on screen.
  • Use the ring alone at small sizes (favicons, avatars).

Don't

  • Recolour the wordmark or the ring dots.
  • Stretch, rotate, outline, or add shadows.
  • Place the dark wordmark over photography or busy patterns.
  • Set the name in title case: it is always lowercase bodywise.
  • Redraw the ring with evenly-sized dots; the size alternation is intentional.

Proportions

05 Motifs & shape

The shapes of Bodywise

The cycle ring

16 dots, alternating Orange (larger) and Berry (smaller). Used as logo mark, section ornament, and watermark.

The dot divider

Four alternating dots introduce centred moments: pull quotes, newsletter, closing sections.

The arch

Doorway-shaped portrait crops for people. Soft circles and generous 24 px card radii everywhere else. No sharp corners, ever.

06 Components

Building blocks

Buttons

Book a Call Work With Me About Book now

Fully rounded. Berry for the primary action, one per screen.

Chips & pills

Birth control options Crisis support Best for Ages 16+

Pills replace long bullet lists; small blush chips tag audiences and facts.

Callout

Do not rely on an app for birth control without making your own body observations.

Blush field, berry left rule. For warnings and important notes.

Pull quote

You are the boss of your own body.

One key sentence per section, lifted in Fraunces italic Berry.

07 Imagery

Two image languages

Photography — warm, human, daylight

  • Natural light, warm wood, plants, lived-in spaces.
  • Laura looks at the camera: approachable, smiling, present.
  • Nature stands in for cycles and seasons (blossoms, sky, mountains).

Illustration — the watercolor set

Hand-painted watercolors add softness and metaphor (fruit cross-sections, transformation, growth). Use on Cream/Blush panels; never overlay text on them.

08 Voice & tone

How Bodywise speaks

  • Speak to "you." Direct, second-person, one reader at a time.
  • Empower, never prescribe. "Only YOU know what is best for you."
  • Gender-expansive always. "People with menstrual cycles," "breast/chest feeding."
  • Honest about limits. "I cannot make your body magically do what you want!"
  • Evidence with warmth. Facts and efficacy numbers, delivered like a friend would.
  • Plain punctuation. Short sentences. Commas and periods; no em dashes.

Signature passage

You are welcome here if you align with the divine feminine.

You are welcome here if you are masculine of centre.

You are welcome here if you are non-binary.

You are welcome if you are gender expansive, or if your gender expression is often in flux.
